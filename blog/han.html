Byung-Chul Han: Philosophy of Exhaustion

2026.02.28 · Philosophy

Overview

Byung-Chul Han diagnoses a paradox at the heart of contemporary life: freedom that exhausts, autonomy that enslaves, and a civilization collapsing under the weight of its own potential. In works like Burnout Society and Psychopolitics, Han reveals how modern capitalism operates not through external coercion but through internalized compulsion.

This essay examines Han's critique of achievement society, the mechanisms of psychopolitics as governance through desire, and the erosion of contemplative life through acceleration. Rather than treating his work as mere pessimism, the analysis explores how Han's philosophy opens space for thinking about resistance through the recovery of time, attention, and genuine otherness.

Core Framework

Burnout Society

Freedom to optimize becomes compulsion. Rest is laziness. Limits are defeat. Self-responsibility as invisible cage.

Psychopolitics

Power through desire, not prohibition. Algorithms steer. Choices constrain. Freedom is governance.

Acceleration

Time colonized. Contemplation vanishes. Every moment optimized. Thought becomes unaffordable.
